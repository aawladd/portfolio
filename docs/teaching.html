Teaching

Research-led teaching, mentorship, and technical learning pathways

My teaching approach connects conceptual depth with implementation, experimentation, and independent thinking. I aim to help students move from foundational understanding toward research readiness and technical confidence.

Teaching philosophy

From theory to research practice

I value structured explanation, conceptual clarity, and project-oriented learning. In areas such as simulation, robotics, machine learning, scientific computing, and control, students benefit most when theory is connected to code, experiments, analysis, and reflection.

My goal is to help students understand not only how to implement a method, but also how to reason about assumptions, evaluate evidence, and communicate results in a scholarly way.

Teaching strengths
  • Concept-first technical explanation
  • Research-informed classroom design
  • Hands-on computational and experimental learning
  • Mentorship for student inquiry and project development
