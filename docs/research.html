Research vision

Connecting physical insight with computational intelligence

My research centers on building methods that combine scientific structure, simulation-based reasoning, and data-driven modeling. I am interested in problems where purely empirical learning is not enough and where computational models benefit from physical knowledge, domain constraints, and control-oriented thinking.

This direction is especially relevant to robotics, forecasting, scientific computing, and intelligent engineering systems, where both reliability and performance matter.

Method pillars
  • Simulation-aware and physics-aware machine learning
  • Robotics experiments connected to modeling and control
  • Deep learning for structured scientific and engineering data
  • Control-theoretic thinking for reliable intelligent systems

Positioning statement

Suggested formal text for your website

My research investigates how numerical simulation, machine learning, deep learning, scientific machine learning, robotics, and control theory can be integrated to develop more reliable and practically useful intelligent systems. I am particularly interested in methods that preserve scientific structure, improve decision quality, and support real-world engineering applications through a balance of mathematical rigor and computational effectiveness.
