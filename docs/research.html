Research Statement

My research explores how computational intelligence can be made more reliable, interpretable, and useful for engineering systems. I am particularly interested in integrating numerical simulation, machine learning, deep learning, scientific machine learning, robotics, and control theory to address problems where physical structure and data must work together. Through this line of work, I aim to develop methods that are not only accurate and efficient, but also grounded in scientific reasoning and capable of supporting real-world decision-making, autonomous systems, and advanced engineering analysis.
